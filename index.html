Designing a Photography Website

The purpose of this website is to enable people living in mainland China to access Helen's grandparents photography website since they only have a Facebook page that is rarely updated and is inaccessible to people who live in countries where Facebook is blocked. Our project will be about us designing and creating a photography website for our client.

    • How can I plan a website? What is the design process for creating websites? What are wireframes, storyboards and prototypes?

    • You can plan a website by using flowcharts, wireframes and storyboards to get the basic feel of your website.
    • For the designing process, you create a flowchart for the amount of pages that you want to make, then you create a wireframe to plot out where you would like things to go. Storyboards are used to flesh out the wireframe and is usually the same as your finished product. You use the storyboard as a base for your website so that you know how things will look before your website is complete.

    Look and Feel

    Our website has a professional art gallery esque feeling. We wanted to capture the photos and make the website look almost like an authentic photo museum or something along those lines. We played around with the background and the style to get the theme and feel that we wanted. We also wanted to make the website convenient to browse photos with.

    Fireworks vs. Dreamweaver

    In terms of making a wireframe, storyboard and general designing, I definitely find Fireworks a lot easier to work with since I have more experience using it than Dreamweaver. It's also a lot better suited for designing and truly capturing an image than Dreamweaver since Dreamweaver's options in terms of designing are definitely limited in my perspective. Dreamweaver is a lot better to use for a functioning website and for coding things into your website and getting it online.

    I think my preference between Fireworks and Dreamweaver vary depending on what I am trying to accomplish. If I'm just making a wirefreame or storyboard, then I'd rather go with Fireworks. If it's to create the final product, then Dreamweaver is the way to go.

    Navigation

    The navigational buttons can be created on Fireworks using the vector and text tools. To create an interactive button on Fireworks, you must first turn the buttons into a symbol on Fireworks.

    From there, you can add more things to your button, such as having them fade when your cursor hovers over the button or you can have them be pushed in when you click the button. Afterwards, you can export the button to Dreamweaver and make an AP div for the buttons. Afterwards, you are able to use the buttons on your planning site.

    The shadow affect underneath the buttons can give the buttons a more 3D effect and have it stand out more. This also makes these pictures look more like buttons.
